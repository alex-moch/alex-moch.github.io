Research and Publications.

My research focuses on creating more resource efficient schemes.

My doctoral research was dedicated to provable security in the random oracle model. Here, some building blocks were replaced with perfectly random counterparts, which then allowed to prove lower bounds on security. This demonstrated the absence of generic attacks.

The big picture is to find small schemes with good security properties. These can then be implemented in hardware with little implementation cost.

Based on that research, we proposed a stream cipher called DRACO. Currently, I am working on stream ciphers that can withstand attacks by an adversary with a quantum computer.
